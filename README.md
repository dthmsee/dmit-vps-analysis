## Breaking Down DMIT's Plan Tiers

DMIT organizes its products into three distinct network tiers, each targeting a different type of buyer. Understanding these tiers is the most important part of choosing the right plan.

### Tier 1 (T1) — Standard International Routing

The entry-level tier. Uses standard BGP routing with no special optimizations for China traffic. Think of this as a regular international VPS — perfectly fine for audiences outside China, and significantly cheaper than the premium tiers.

Hong Kong Tier 1 plans start at around **$3/month**, making them among the most affordable Hong Kong-based VPS options available from a reputable provider.

### Eyeball (EB) — CMIN2 Optimized

The middle tier, built around CMIN2 (China Mobile International's optimized network). This routing benefits China Mobile and Unicom users specifically, offering noticeably better performance to those networks than standard BGP without paying full CN2 GIA prices.

The LAX.EB (Los Angeles Eyeball) series is the main product in this tier.

### Premium / Pro — CN2 GIA + AS9929 + CMI

The top tier. These plans use a combination of CN2 GIA (China Telecom), AS9929 (China Unicom's elite backbone), and CMI (China Mobile International). All three major Chinese carriers get premium-grade routing simultaneously. This is what serious operators use when latency and reliability to China is non-negotiable.

Available in Los Angeles (LAX.Pro), Hong Kong (HKG.Pro), and Tokyo (TYO.Pro).
